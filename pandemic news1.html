Philippine COVID-19 positivity rate could rise by April, May â€”UP doctor


Published March 21, 2021 12:32pm by CONSUELO MARQUEZ , GMA News

The positivity rate of COVID-19 cases in the Philippines could still increase in succeeding months depending on the interventions of the government, a university expert on pandemic response said Sunday.

Professor Jomar Rabajante of the University of the Philippines Pandemic Response Team said the country's current positivity rate, especially in Metro Manila, has gone up to 15%.

Positivity rate is the percentage of those who test positive from the total number of people tested for the coronavirus, he added.

"Dati bumababa tayo ng less than 10, ngayon sobrang taas, 15 percent, lalo na sa NCR," said Rabajante on Dobol B TV. [Positivity rate before is 10 % but now it is increasing to 15 percent especially in NCR.]

Based on our monitoring, and given our evidence, the positivity rate could increase possibly in April and May, he said in Filipino.


"Sa ngayon, siyempre ang aming mga monitoring depende kung ano ang mangyayari sa susunod na araw pero ngayon given ang evidence na nakukuha nating datos, posibleng matagalan, posibleng umabot ng April or May, depende sa interventions ng government."


Rabajante also said that eased restrictions on mobility could be one of the causes in the surge of COVID-19 infections.
