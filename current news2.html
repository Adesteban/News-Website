Gov't should have been alarmed by 5,000 new COVID-19 cases –Sotto


Published March 20, 2021 7:38pm by JAMIL SANTOS, GMA News

Senate President Vicente Sotto III on Saturday said the Inter-Agency Task Force on Emerging Infectious Diseases (IATF) and the Department of Health (DOH) should have been alarmed when new COVID-19 cases in the country reached 5,000.

"Tingin ko merong mga kakulangan ang diskarte if not may pagkakamali. Tingin ko malaki ang kakulangan," Sotto said over a radio interview.

(I think there are deficiencies in strategy, if not mistakes. I think there is a huge inadequacy.)

Sotto said he was not surprised when new COVID-19 cases reached 3,000 every day, due to the number of people having their tests for COVID-19.

"Pero nu'ng umabot ng 4,000 o 5,000 dapat naalarma ang DOH. Dapat naalarma ang executive department, IATF, dapat gumawa na ng paraan," he said.
